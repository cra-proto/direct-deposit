Payments the CRA sends you

Payments the CRA sends you - Direct deposit for Part XIII non-resident withholding tax

If you are a non-resident of Canada filing Form NR7-R, Application for Refund of Part XIII Tax Withheld or you have a non-resident tax account, you can request the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) to issue your refund by direct deposit.

On this page

  • If you are not impacted by Part XIII tax withheld
  • How to request direct deposit
  • If you do not have a Canadian bank account
  • Contact us

If you are not impacted by Part XIII tax withheld

The instructions on this page are only for people dealing with Part XIII tax withheld. If you are a non-resident and this doesn't apply to you, follow the instructions for setting up direct deposit on one of the following pages:

  • Non-resident individuals: Direct deposit for individuals
  • Non-resident businesses: Direct deposit for businesses
  • Non-resident trusts: Direct deposit for trusts

How to request direct deposit

Form NR7-R applicant

If you request a refund using Form NR7-R, you can receive your payment by direct deposit if all of the following conditions are met:

  • You have a Canadian bank account
  • The name on your bank account matches your name (as the applicant) on Form NR7-R

Follow these steps to have your refund issued by direct deposit.

  1. Fill out Form NR7-R, Application for Refund of Part XIII Tax Withheld
  2. Fill out Form NR304, Direct Deposit Request for Non-Resident Account Holders and NR7-R Refund Applicants
  3. Submit your forms:
    Online
    Submit your forms through your CRA account
    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your Individual account to access My Account
    3. Select Submit documents
    4. Under "Do you have a case/reference number?" select No
    5. In the drop down "Topic" list, select Part XIII tax for non-residents
    6. Under "Select the reason for your submission," select Send Form NR7-R
    7. Attach Form NR7-R and Form NR304
    By mail
    Send your forms by mail

    Attach both forms together and mail to:

    Non-Resident Withholding Section
    Canada Revenue Agency
    P.O. Box 20000, Station A
    Sudbury ON
    P3A 5C1

    By fax

Non-resident tax account holder

If you are the owner or legal representative of a non-resident tax account, you can use direct deposit to receive your payments if all of the following conditions are met:

  • You have a Canadian bank account
  • You have a non-resident tax account starting with NR followed by an alpha character and 6 digits
  • The name on your bank account matches the name on your non-resident tax account

Follow these steps to have your refund issued by direct deposit.

  1. Fill out Form NR304, Direct Deposit Request for Non-Resident Account Holders and NR7-R Refund Applicants
  2. Submit your form:
    Online
    Submit your form through your CRA account
    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your Non-resident tax account
    3. Select Submit documents
    4. Attach Form NR304
    By mail

If you do not have a Canadian bank account

Direct deposit is only available with Canadian banks and credit unions. If we send you a cheque and you are unable to cash it in the country you are living in, you may request to have your payment wire transferred into a foreign bank account.

If you have the cheque or already returned it

Fill out Form NR304, Direct Deposit Request for Non-Resident Account Holders and NR7-R Refund Applicants.

The CRA needs this information each time you return a cheque to have the payment reissued in a different currency or by wire transfer.

Depending on the country you live in, some banks may require additional information to be included to process a wire transfer. We suggest that you contact your bank to determine if any additional requirements are needed and include them in a letter.

Send the form, and the cheque and letter (if applicable) to:

Non-Resident Withholding Section
Canada Revenue Agency
Post Office Box 20000, Station A
Sudbury ON P3A 5C1
Canada

If you lost or did not receive the cheque

If your cheque was lost, destroyed, stolen, or is missing, call the CRA to request a replacement. A phone agent will do a verification, which may include sending forms for you to fill out. A replacement cheque will be issued, if warranted.
